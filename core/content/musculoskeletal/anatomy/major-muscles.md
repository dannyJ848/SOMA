# Major Muscles

## Overview
The human body contains approximately 600 skeletal muscles, comprising 40-50% of body weight. Muscles produce movement, maintain posture, stabilize joints, and generate heat. This section covers the major muscles organized by body region, including their origins, insertions, actions, and innervations.

---

## Head and Neck Muscles

### Level 1: Foundation
Head and neck muscles control facial expression, chewing, eye movement, and neck movement.

**Major Groups:**
- Muscles of facial expression
- Muscles of mastication (chewing)
- Eye muscles
- Neck muscles

### Level 2: Core Understanding

**Muscles of Facial Expression:**
All innervated by facial nerve (CN VII)

| Muscle | Location | Action |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Frontalis | Forehead | Raises eyebrows |
| Orbicularis oculi | Around eye | Closes eyelids |
| Orbicularis oris | Around mouth | Closes/purses lips |
| Buccinator | Cheek | Compresses cheek |
| Zygomaticus major | Cheek | Elevates mouth angle (smile) |

**Muscles of Mastication:**
All innervated by trigeminal nerve (CN V3 - mandibular division)

| Muscle | Origin | Insertion | Action |
|--------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Masseter | Zygomatic arch | Mandible angle | Elevates mandible |
| Temporalis | Temporal fossa | Coronoid process | Elevates, retracts mandible |
| Lateral pterygoid | Sphenoid | Mandible condyle | Protrudes, opens mandible |
| Medial pterygoid | Sphenoid, maxilla | Mandible angle | Elevates, protrudes mandible |

### Level 3: Clinical Application

**Neck Muscles:**

**Superficial:**
| Muscle | Origin | Insertion | Action | Innervation |
|--------|--------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Platysma | Clavicle, fascia | Mandible, skin | Depresses mandible | CN VII |
| Sternocleidomastoid | Sternum, clavicle | Mastoid process | Flexes, rotates head | CN XI |
| Trapezius | Skull, spine | Clavicle, scapula | Elevates, retracts scapula | CN XI |

**Suprahyoid muscles** (elevate hyoid/larynx for swallowing):
- Digastric, stylohyoid, mylohyoid, geniohyoid

**Infrahyoid muscles** (depress hyoid/larynx):
- Sternohyoid, sternothyroid, thyrohyoid, omohyoid

**Clinical Correlations:**
- Torticollis: SCM spasm/shortening
- CN VII palsy: Facial asymmetry, inability to close eye
- CN XI injury: Shoulder droop, weak head rotation

### Level 4: Advanced Mechanisms

**Scalene Muscles:**
- Anterior, middle, posterior
- Origin: Cervical transverse processes
- Insertion: Ribs 1-2
- Action: Elevate ribs, lateral neck flexion
- Clinical: Thoracic outlet syndrome, scalene blocks

**Deep Neck Flexors:**
- Longus colli, longus capitis
- Rectus capitis anterior/lateralis
- Important for neck stability
- Weakness associated with neck pain

**Suboccipital Triangle:**
- Rectus capitis posterior major/minor
- Obliquus capitis superior/inferior
- Contains: Vertebral artery, suboccipital nerve (C1)

## Trunk Muscles

### Level 1: Foundation
Trunk muscles move the spine, assist breathing, and protect abdominal organs.

**Major Groups:**
- Back muscles
- Abdominal muscles
- Pelvic floor muscles
- Respiratory muscles

### Level 2: Core Understanding

**Abdominal Muscles:**

| Muscle | Fiber Direction | Action |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| Rectus abdominis | Vertical | Flexes trunk |
| External oblique | Inferiomedial | Rotates, flexes trunk |
| Internal oblique | Superiomedial | Rotates, flexes trunk |
| Transversus abdominis | Horizontal | Compresses abdomen |

**Superficial Back Muscles:**
| Muscle | Action | Innervation |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Trapezius | Elevates, retracts, rotates scapula | CN XI |
| Latissimus dorsi | Extends, adducts, medially rotates arm | Thoracodorsal |
| Rhomboid major/minor | Retracts scapula | Dorsal scapular |
| Levator scapulae | Elevates scapula | Dorsal scapular, C3-4 |

### Level 3: Clinical Application

**Erector Spinae Group:**
Three columns running vertically along spine:
- **Iliocostalis**: Lateral (ribs)
- **Longissimus**: Middle (transverse processes)
- **Spinalis**: Medial (spinous processes)

Action: Extend and laterally flex spine
Innervation: Posterior rami of spinal nerves

**Transversospinalis Group:**
- **Semispinalis**: Spans 4-6 segments
- **Multifidus**: Spans 2-4 segments, key spinal stabilizer
- **Rotatores**: Spans 1-2 segments

**Respiratory Muscles:**
Primary:
- Diaphragm: Main inspiratory muscle (phrenic nerve C3-5)
- External intercostals: Elevate ribs (inspiration)
- Internal intercostals: Depress ribs (forced expiration)

Accessory:
- SCM, scalenes, pectoralis minor (inspiration)
- Abdominal muscles (forced expiration)

### Level 4: Advanced Mechanisms

**Core Stability System:**
1. **Diaphragm**: Top
2. **Pelvic floor**: Bottom
3. **Transversus abdominis**: Front/sides
4. **Multifidus**: Back

Function: Creates intra-abdominal pressure for spinal stability

**Quadratus Lumborum:**
- Origin: Iliac crest
- Insertion: 12th rib, lumbar transverse processes
- Action: Lateral flexion, hip hiking
- Innervation: T12-L4

**Psoas Major:**
- Origin: T12-L5 vertebral bodies and discs
- Insertion: Lesser trochanter (with iliacus)
- Action: Hip flexion, trunk flexion
- Innervation: L1-L3
- Clinical: Psoas abscess, hip flexor tightness

### Level 5: Expert Integration

**Thoracolumbar Fascia:**
- Three layers surrounding back muscles
- Attachment for transversus abdominis, latissimus dorsi
- Force transmission between upper and lower body
- Important for lifting mechanics

**Pelvic Floor Muscles:**
- Levator ani: Pubococcygeus, puborectalis, iliococcygeus
- Coccygeus
- Functions: Support pelvic organs, continence, sexual function

**Abdominal Wall Layers:**
Superficial to deep:
1. Skin
2. Superficial fascia (Camper's, Scarpa's)
3. External oblique
4. Internal oblique
5. Transversus abdominis
6. Transversalis fascia
7. Preperitoneal fat
8. Parietal peritoneum

## Upper Limb Muscles

### Level 1: Foundation
Upper limb muscles enable shoulder movement, elbow flexion/extension, and hand function.

**Regions:**
- Shoulder/rotator cuff muscles
- Arm muscles
- Forearm muscles
- Hand muscles

### Level 2: Core Understanding

**Rotator Cuff (SITS):**
| Muscle | Origin | Insertion | Action | Innervation |
|--------|--------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Supraspinatus | Supraspinous fossa | Greater tubercle | Abduction (first 15Â°) | Suprascapular |
| Infraspinatus | Infraspinous fossa | Greater tubercle | External rotation | Suprascapular |
| Teres minor | Lateral scapula | Greater tubercle | External rotation | Axillary |
| Subscapularis | Subscapular fossa | Lesser tubercle | Internal rotation | Subscapular |

**Arm Muscles:**

Anterior Compartment (Musculocutaneous nerve):
| Muscle | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Biceps brachii | Flexes elbow, supinates forearm |
| Brachialis | Flexes elbow (strongest) |
| Coracobrachialis | Flexes, adducts arm |

Posterior Compartment (Radial nerve):
| Muscle | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Triceps brachii | Extends elbow |
| Anconeus | Extends elbow |

### Level 3: Clinical Application

**Shoulder Muscles:**
| Muscle | Action | Innervation |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Deltoid | Abducts arm | Axillary |
| Pectoralis major | Adducts, flexes, internally rotates | Pectoral nerves |
| Latissimus dorsi | Extends, adducts, internally rotates | Thoracodorsal |
| Teres major | Extends, adducts, internally rotates | Lower subscapular |

**Clinical Tests:**
- Empty can test: Supraspinatus
- External rotation against resistance: Infraspinatus, teres minor
- Lift-off test: Subscapularis
- Speed's test: Biceps tendon

**Nerve Injuries:**
| Nerve | Muscles Affected | Deficit |
|-------|-----------------|---------|
| Axillary | Deltoid, teres minor | Cannot abduct arm |
| Musculocutaneous | Biceps, brachialis | Weak elbow flexion |
| Radial | Triceps, wrist extensors | Wrist drop |
| Median | Forearm flexors, thenar | Weak grip, thenar wasting |
| Ulnar | Interossei, hypothenar | Claw hand |

### Level 4: Advanced Mechanisms

**Forearm Anterior Compartment:**
Superficial:
- Pronator teres, flexor carpi radialis, palmaris longus, flexor carpi ulnaris, flexor digitorum superficialis

Deep:
- Flexor digitorum profundus, flexor pollicis longus, pronator quadratus

**Forearm Posterior Compartment:**
Superficial:
- Brachioradialis, extensor carpi radialis longus/brevis, extensor digitorum, extensor digiti minimi, extensor carpi ulnaris

Deep:
- Supinator, abductor pollicis longus, extensor pollicis brevis/longus, extensor indicis

**Hand Intrinsic Muscles:**

Thenar (Median nerve):
- Abductor pollicis brevis, flexor pollicis brevis (superficial), opponens pollicis

Hypothenar (Ulnar nerve):
- Abductor digiti minimi, flexor digiti minimi, opponens digiti minimi

Interossei (Ulnar nerve):
- Palmar: Adduct fingers (PAD)
- Dorsal: Abduct fingers (DAB)

Lumbricals:
- L1-2: Median nerve
- L3-4: Ulnar nerve
- Flex MCP, extend IP joints

### Level 5: Expert Integration

**Hand Function:**
- Power grip: Extrinsic flexors + intrinsic muscles
- Precision grip: Thenar muscles + interossei + lumbricals
- Ulnar nerve: "Musician's nerve" (fine motor control)
- Median nerve: "Eye of the hand" (sensory)

**Carpal Tunnel Contents:**
- Median nerve (most superficial)
- Flexor pollicis longus (1)
- Flexor digitorum superficialis (4)
- Flexor digitorum profundus (4)
- Total: 10 structures

## Lower Limb Muscles

### Level 1: Foundation
Lower limb muscles enable standing, walking, running, and jumping.

**Regions:**
- Hip muscles
- Thigh muscles
- Leg muscles
- Foot muscles

### Level 2: Core Understanding

**Hip Muscles:**

Gluteal region:
| Muscle | Action | Innervation |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Gluteus maximus | Hip extension, external rotation | Inferior gluteal |
| Gluteus medius | Hip abduction, internal rotation | Superior gluteal |
| Gluteus minimus | Hip abduction, internal rotation | Superior gluteal |

Iliopsoas (Hip flexor):
- Psoas major + iliacus
- Strongest hip flexor
- Innervation: Femoral nerve, L1-3

### Level 3: Clinical Application

**Thigh Muscles:**

Anterior Compartment (Femoral nerve):
| Muscle | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis/medialis/intermedius) | Extend knee |
| Sartorius | Flex, abduct, externally rotate hip |

Medial Compartment (Obturator nerve):
| Muscle | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Adductor magnus, longus, brevis | Adduct hip |
| Gracilis | Adducts hip, flexes knee |
| Pectineus | Adducts, flexes hip |

Posterior Compartment (Sciatic nerve):
| Muscle | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Biceps femoris | Flexes knee, extends hip |
| Semitendinosus | Flexes knee, extends hip |
| Semimembranosus | Flexes knee, extends hip |

**Clinical Tests:**
- Trendelenburg test: Gluteus medius weakness
- Thomas test: Hip flexor tightness
- Ober test: IT band tightness

### Level 4: Advanced Mechanisms

**Leg Muscles:**

Anterior Compartment (Deep peroneal nerve):
| Muscle | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Tibialis anterior | Dorsiflexion, inversion |
| Extensor hallucis longus | Extends great toe |
| Extensor digitorum longus | Extends toes 2-5 |

Lateral Compartment (Superficial peroneal nerve):
| Muscle | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Peroneus longus | Eversion, plantarflexion |
| Peroneus brevis | Eversion, plantarflexion |

Posterior Compartment (Tibial nerve):

Superficial:
- Gastrocnemius: Plantarflexion, knee flexion
- Soleus: Plantarflexion
- Plantaris: Weak plantarflexion

Deep:
- Tibialis posterior: Plantarflexion, inversion
- Flexor digitorum longus: Flexes toes 2-5
- Flexor hallucis longus: Flexes great toe

**Compartment Syndrome:**
- Four compartments in leg
- Anterior most commonly affected
- "5 Ps": Pain, pallor, pulselessness, paresthesias, paralysis
- Emergency fasciotomy required

### Level 5: Expert Integration

**Foot Intrinsic Muscles:**

Layer 1 (superficial):
- Abductor hallucis, flexor digitorum brevis, abductor digiti minimi

Layer 2:
- Quadratus plantae, lumbricals

Layer 3:
- Flexor hallucis brevis, adductor hallucis, flexor digiti minimi brevis

Layer 4 (deep):
- Interossei (plantar and dorsal)

**Gait Muscles:**

| Phase | Primary Muscles |
|-------|-----------------|
| Heel strike | Tibialis anterior (eccentric) |
| Loading response | Quadriceps |
| Midstance | Gluteus medius, hip abductors |
| Terminal stance | Gastrocnemius, soleus |
| Pre-swing | Hip flexors |
| Swing | Hip flexors, hamstrings (late) |

**Nerve Injuries:**

| Nerve | Loss | Gait Pattern |
|-------|------|--------------|
| Femoral | Knee extension | Knee buckling |
| Obturator | Hip adduction | Leg swings laterally |
| Superior gluteal | Hip abduction | Trendelenburg gait |
| Inferior gluteal | Hip extension | Difficulty climbing stairs |
| Common peroneal | Dorsiflexion, eversion | Foot drop, steppage gait |
| Tibial | Plantarflexion | Toe walking difficulty |

## Key Clinical Pearls

1. **Rotator cuff tears**: Most common is supraspinatus
2. **Wrist drop**: Radial nerve injury (spiral groove of humerus)
3. **Foot drop**: Common peroneal nerve injury (fibular head)
4. **Trendelenburg sign**: Gluteus medius weakness
5. **Core stability**: Transversus abdominis and multifidus are key

---

## Summary

Skeletal muscles work in coordinated groups to produce movement and maintain posture. Understanding muscle origins, insertions, actions, and innervations is essential for diagnosing weakness, interpreting physical examination findings, and planning rehabilitation. The relationship between nerves and muscles is particularly important for localizing lesions and predicting deficits.
